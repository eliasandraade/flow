# Flow — Product Overview

**Authors:** Elias Sales de Freitas · João Vitor Bernardo · FIAP

---

## The Problem

Most organisations have no shortage of ideas. Employees identify operational inefficiencies, spot market opportunities, and propose improvements constantly. The problem is what happens next — which is usually nothing.

Ideas get lost in email threads. Decisions are made verbally with no record of the reasoning. Projects start without clear ownership. Nobody knows which initiatives are blocked, for how long, or by whom. ROI is never measured because nobody knows where to look.

The result is a trust problem: employees stop submitting ideas because they believe nothing will come of them, and leadership cannot demonstrate that the innovation process produces any value.

---

## The Solution

Flow provides a governed pipeline from idea to outcome.

Every idea goes through a defined lifecycle — creation, review, approval, project execution, and result measurement. Every transition is recorded. Every decision carries a reason. Every blocked project is visible. Every outcome is quantified.

Flow does not try to replace creativity with process. It provides just enough structure to ensure that good ideas reach execution, that decisions are accountable, and that results are measurable.

---

## Value Proposition

| Without Flow | With Flow |
|-------------|-----------|
| Ideas are submitted and forgotten | Every idea has a visible status |
| Approval decisions are verbal and untracked | Every approval and rejection is recorded with reasoning |
| Nobody knows which projects are blocked | Blocked projects are surfaced immediately with duration and reason |
| ROI is estimated once and never revisited | Estimated and actual ROI are tracked independently |
| Leadership has no operational visibility | A live dashboard shows all key metrics in one view |
| No accountability for decisions | Full audit trail — who did what, when, and why |

## User Roles

Flow has three distinct roles. Each role has a focused, purposeful interface.

### Operator
The idea generator. An Operator is any employee who wants to contribute ideas to the organisation.

**What they can do:**
- Submit ideas with a title, problem statement, and description
- Track the status of their own ideas (Draft, Under Review, Approved, Rejected)
- Submit a Draft idea for manager review
- View feedback from managers on rejected ideas
- Earn points for participation (gamification)

**What they cannot do:**
- See other users' ideas
- Approve or manage projects
- View the dashboard

---

### Manager
The decision maker and execution owner. A Manager reviews ideas, decides what gets built, and controls project execution.

**What they can do:**
- Review all submitted ideas
- Approve ideas (with optional comment) or reject them (with mandatory reason)
- Convert approved ideas into projects
- Manage the full project lifecycle (start, complete, block, unblock, cancel)
- Record project results — estimated and actual ROI
- View the operations dashboard

**What they cannot do:**
- Submit their own ideas (separate role responsibility)
- View other users' personal point scores without explicit access

---

### Leadership
The strategic observer. Leadership has read-only access to the high-level view of the entire innovation pipeline.

**What they can do:**
- View the operations dashboard with all key metrics
- See blocked projects — title, reason, and days blocked
- Monitor conversion rates, ROI totals, and bottleneck indicators

**What they cannot do:**
- Create or manage ideas or projects
- Approve decisions

---

## Core Pipeline

```
Operator creates idea
        ↓
Idea submitted for review
        ↓
Manager reviews → Approved or Rejected (with reason)
        ↓
Approved idea converted to Project
        ↓
Project executed (Planning → InProgress → Completed)
        ↓
Result recorded (ROI: estimated + actual)
        ↓
Leadership monitors via Dashboard
```

Every step in this pipeline is audited. Nothing disappears. No decision is anonymous.
